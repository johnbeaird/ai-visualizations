Slideshow Guide

How to build a slideshow that will make the room go quiet.

🛠 Recommended Tools (Free)
  • 🎞 Canva (free) — Most polished, easy drag-and-drop, built-in transitions, music overlay
  • 📊 Google Slides — Simple, works on any device, loop on TV easily
  • 🎬 iMovie / CapCut — If you want a video with music built in (most emotional option)
  • 📸 Apple Photos Slideshow — Dead simple if all photos are in iPhone camera roll
📖 Suggested Slideshow Arc (~10 minutes)
  1. "Where it started" — Early photos of Cindy, maybe even pre-career / early Clark County years (1994–2000)
  2. "The Work" — Photos from Clark County, colleagues, team events, any photos from foster care work (2000–2015)
  3. "The People Who Showed Up With Her" — Coworkers, friends from work, Kris, team photos
  4. "Home Base" — John, family, The BigHouse — the person she came home to every night after hard days
  5. "30 Years Later" — Recent photos, what she's built, what's next (cruise! retirement!)
  6. Final slide: "Thank you for showing up — for 30 years. We love you, Cindy." with everyone's names
🎵 Music Suggestions
  • 🎸 "Here Comes the Sun" — Beatles (warm opener)
  • 🎹 "Beautiful Day" — U2 (builds energy)
  • 🎵 "Good Riddance (Time of Your Life)" — Green Day (classic nostalgia)
  • 🎶 "The House That Built Me" — Miranda Lambert (emotional closer)
  • 🎤 Ask Kris what Cindy's favorite songs are — personal beats generic every time
📸 How to Collect Photos Fast
  • 📁 Create a shared Google Photos album or Dropbox folder and share the link with Cindy's Clark County coworkers
  • 📱 Text Kris and a few key colleagues directly — give them a 2-week deadline
  • 🏛️ Ask Cindy's department if they have any official team photos or event photos from over the years
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Don't forget family photos — Cindy at home is just as important as Cindy at work
